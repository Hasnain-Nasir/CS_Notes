4.8 Feasibility Study

← Topic 4

A feasibility study evaluates whether a proposed software project is worth pursuing before the organisation commits significant resources. It is typically conducted during project initiation in the project management process and as the first step of the RE process in §4.2.

Four types of feasibility

  • Technical feasibility: Can the required technology, skills, and infrastructure support the proposed system? Are there proven solutions or must unproven technology be used?
  • Economic feasibility: Do the expected benefits (cost savings, revenue, efficiency) justify the development and operational costs? A cost–benefit analysis or return-on-investment calculation is performed.
  • Operational feasibility: Will the organisation and its users accept and effectively use the new system? Are workflows compatible? Is adequate training available?
  • Schedule feasibility: Can the system be delivered within the required timeframe given available resources and estimated effort?

Additional feasibility dimensions

  • Legal/regulatory feasibility: Does the system comply with applicable laws, data protection rules, and industry regulations?
  • Social feasibility: Will the system have acceptable social or ethical impacts on employees, customers, or society?

Feasibility study process

  1. Define the project scope and objectives at a high level.
  2. Identify alternative solutions (build, buy, outsource, do nothing).
  3. Evaluate each alternative against the feasibility dimensions.
  4. Estimate rough cost, schedule, and resource needs (link to Topic 3 §3.5).
  5. Prepare a feasibility report with a recommended option and go/no-go decision.

Output and decision

The feasibility report informs the project charter and the management decision to proceed, defer, or cancel the project. A "no-go" at this stage saves far more cost than discovering infeasibility during design or coding.

Exam question — Feasibility study

Q: What is a feasibility study? Describe the four main types and the decision it supports.

Model answer points:

  • Define feasibility study as pre-commitment evaluation of project viability.
  • Describe technical, economic, operational, and schedule feasibility.
  • Mention legal/regulatory and social dimensions.
  • Outline process: scope → alternatives → evaluate → estimate → report → go/no-go.
  • Link to PM initiation phase and RE process first step.
