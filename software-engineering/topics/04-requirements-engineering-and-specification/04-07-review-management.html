4.7 Review & Management of User Needs

← Topic 4

Requirements review and requirements management ensure that documented requirements are correct, complete, and agreed upon, and that they remain controlled as the project evolves. Together they protect the requirements baseline throughout the SDLC.

Requirements review (validation)

Review is a structured examination of the requirements document to find defects before design begins:

  • Formal inspections: A trained team systematically checks the SRS against a checklist (completeness, consistency, testability).
  • Walkthroughs: The author presents the document to stakeholders who ask questions and suggest corrections.
  • Prototyping: Stakeholders interact with a mock-up to confirm that requirements match their expectations.
  • Test-case generation: If test cases cannot be written for a requirement, it is probably ambiguous or untestable.
  • Consistency checks: Models (DFD, ERD) are compared with the written SRS to detect contradictions.

Requirements management

Management continues after the baseline is approved and covers the full project lifecycle:

  • Version control: Each revision of the requirements document is numbered and stored so the team always knows which version is current.
  • Change control: Proposed changes are logged, assessed for impact on cost, schedule, and design, and approved or rejected by a change control board (CCB). This aligns with SPM scope management in Topic 3 §3.9.
  • Traceability: Each requirement is linked forward to design elements, code modules, and test cases, and backward to its elicitation source.
  • Status tracking: Requirements are marked as proposed, approved, implemented, verified, or deferred.
  • Impact analysis: Before accepting a change, the team evaluates which design, code, and test artefacts are affected.

Managing user needs over time

  • User needs evolve as the business environment changes; the management process must accommodate controlled change without losing stability.
  • Prioritisation (MoSCoW or ranking) helps decide which new needs fit the current release and which are deferred.
  • Regular stakeholder reviews during development catch drift between implemented software and agreed requirements.
  • Traceability matrices make it visible when a user need has no corresponding design or test, or vice versa.
Exam question — Review and management

Q: Distinguish requirements review from requirements management. Why is each needed?

Model answer points:

  • Define review/validation: structured examination to find defects before design (inspections, walkthroughs, prototyping).
  • Define management: version control, change control, traceability, status tracking, impact analysis.
  • Explain review catches errors early; management handles evolving needs with controlled change.
  • Link change control to SPM scope management and CCB.
  • Mention traceability forward (design/test) and backward (source).
