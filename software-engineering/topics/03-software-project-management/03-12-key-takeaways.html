Key takeaways — exam revision

← Topic 3
  • SPM plans, organises, monitors, and controls software projects to deliver on time, within budget, and to required quality. Software intangibility makes this harder than physical projects.
  • PM process (5 phases): initiation/feasibility (business case, charter, go/no-go) → planning (WBS, schedule, budget, risk, estimation) → execution (SDLC work + SDLC model choice) → monitoring & control (runs alongside execution) → closure (acceptance, lessons learned). Phases 3 & 4 overlap.
  • Planning principles: plan before start, risk analysis upfront, track/update plan, quality standards, flexibility for controlled change.
  • SPM management types: conflict, risk, requirement, change, SCM, release management.
  • Project manager is the central coordinator responsible for planning, team leadership, stakeholder communication, risk management, change control, and delivery.
  • SPM complexities: management-area (time, cost, quality, risk, HR, communication, procurement, integration), technical (infrastructure, deployment, API), and software-specific (invisibility, changeability, interaction, uniqueness, exactness, team work, estimation). Factors: changing requirements, resource/schedule constraints, technical challenges, QA, stakeholders, risks.
  • Four estimation types: post/delayed, base/experience, decomposition (KLOC white-box / FP black-box), empirical (COCOMO).
  • Key formulas: Effort = Size/Productivity; Duration = Effort/Team Size; Cost = Effort × Pay rate.
  • COCOMO (Boehm, 1981, 63 projects): empirical cost model; E = a(KLOC)b, T = c(E)d, team = E/T. Project types: Organic (2–50 KLOC, familiar), Semi-detached (50–300, mixed team), Embedded (300+, complex). Basic constants: organic (2.4, 1.05, 2.5, 0.38), semi (3.0, 1.12, 2.5, 0.35), embedded (3.6, 1.20, 2.5, 0.32).
  • COCOMO levels: Basic (KLOC + mode, rough) → Intermediate (E = a(KLOC)b × EAF; 15 cost drivers in product/hardware/personnel/project categories) → Detailed (per-module across six phases, most accurate).
  • Resource allocation: CPM (critical path), PERT (three time estimates), Gantt charts, resource levelling, Agile sprint allocation.
  • Risk categories: project (schedule/budget), technical (design/performance), business (market/funding).
  • Risk process: identify → analyse → assess → mitigate (avoid/transfer/mitigate/accept) → monitor. Exposure = probability × impact.
  • Monitoring & control: track schedule, cost, scope, quality, resources, risks; take corrective/preventive action; enforce change control.
  • CMM (SEI) five levels: 1 Initial (ad hoc) → 2 Repeatable (basic PM) → 3 Defined (standardised) → 4 Managed (quantitative) → 5 Optimizing (continuous improvement).
  • Quasi-renewal: mathematically Xi = αXi−1 (α=1 → renewal; α>1 → reliability growth); as a process = continuous updates vs full replacement.
