1.7 Need of Software Engineering

← Topic 1

Software Engineering is needed because informal programming is insufficient for modern, large-scale systems. It provides a systematic, scientific, and disciplined approach to develop, operate, and maintain software on computer systems and electronic devices.

Why Software Engineering is needed (detailed)

  • Structured development approach: Software Engineering provides organised design, development, testing, and maintenance activities that improve consistency and quality.
  • Managing complexity: Large problems are broken into smaller, manageable parts so that teams can understand and control the system.
  • Quality and reliability assurance: Standards and testing are applied so that software meets requirements and is as defect-free as possible.
  • Cost and time management: Proper planning avoids wasted work and helps projects finish on schedule and within budget.
  • Proven methodologies: Approaches such as Agile, Scrum, and Waterfall improve productivity and team coordination.
  • Testing and QA: Unit, integration, and acceptance testing help catch defects early before they reach users.
  • Project management and collaboration: Resources, schedules, budgets, and team coordination are managed in a planned way.
  • Maintainability and evolution: Software can be updated more easily as user needs and technology change over time.

Practical benefits (summary)

  • Software Engineering helps teams handle large projects without chaos or uncontrolled changes.
  • It reduces cost by eliminating unnecessary work and rework during development.
  • It saves time because development follows a planned and monitored process.
  • It helps deliver reliable software on schedule instead of relying on ad-hoc coding.
  • It increases productivity by applying testing and quality checks at every level of the system.
Exam question — Need of SE

Q: Explain why Software Engineering is needed in the modern software industry.

Model answer points:

  • The need for Software Engineering is linked to the software crisis, because informal methods fail when projects grow in size and complexity.
  • Software Engineering manages complexity, cost, time, quality, and maintainability through systematic processes.
  • It enables team collaboration and repeatable processes instead of depending on individual coding habits.
  • It should be contrasted with the build-and-fix approach described in §1.6, which works only for small programs.
